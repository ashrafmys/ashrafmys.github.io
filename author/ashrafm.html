Running, Walking & Life

  • by ashrafm
  • on Fri 03 March 2017

There were times when I felt I wanted to walk instead of running. It's not that I didn't have the energy to run. My legs were still capable to keep running. But why, I decided to walk instead of run ?

It comes down to motivation, self-discipline and goal

Motivation

Why do you want to keep running ? Is it because you want to complete within the given time for the medal ? Or is it because you want to beat your personal record ? Or perhaps you have your own personal reason ? Find that motivation and remind yourself of that movitation whenever you feel want to stop.

Self-discipline

For some reasons, your might feel demotivated at certain points during the run. At this point, the only thing that will push you is your self-discipline. You don't stop no matter what because you are one hell of disciplined running mice. You have to run whether you like it or otherwise. Reminding yourself of your strong self-discipline definitely will help.

Goals

One common area in both of the factors is you need some kind of goal. Without the goal, you have nothing to look forward to. Without the goal, no amount of motivation or self-discipline will keep you moving.

All these areas are applicable to life in general.

To keep going in life, you'll need a goal, some motivation and one hell of self-discipline.

Kuala Lumpur City Day Run 2017

  • by ashrafm
  • on Thu 02 March 2017
  • Event : Kuala Lumpur City Day Run 2017
  • Location : Padang Merbok route
  • Date : 26th Feb 2017
  • Start time : 730am
  • Weather : Raining in the beginning

Review

Somehow, I felt this was my worst outdoor running event but yet for some miracle, I did manage to get my best time for this event.

In my previous event, my average pace was 7’35” min/km. This new event however I managed to get 7’08” min/km

Some reflections on this run :

1. Running in the rain sucks. This was my first time running in the rain. It seems more energy was needed to run in this condition – to get rid of stressful feeling running wet, to carry water drenched clothes, to keep your face clear from rain water distraction while running

2. Hilly run. I was not expecting something like this – too many uphills. I walked for all the uphills. If I were to run again on this route, I might be mentally prepared on what should I do. My advice – for uphill run, try to continuously run (slow is OK) but if you can’t, do short bursts. But for downhill: don’t stop, keep running with long stride. This is the key to get better average pace.

3. Motivation to continuously run. During my previous run, the only thing that kept me running continuously was my desire to get the finisher medal and hence I must run within the time limit for the medal. I had no previous reference so I was so scared I might not make it which led to continuos run in the past event.

However, this time was different. I knew, no matter the completion time, I’ll get the medal. The only question was – can I finish within 1 hour 30 mins for E-Cert. To run within this time limitation was the only motivation I had.

Unfortunately, due to many uphills, I was demotivated and thought that I might not make it which led to – I couldn’t be bothered. This led to lots of walking compared to previous event.

I kept going though so that at least I was not that far off from the time limit. To my surprise, I did manage to run 12km within the time limit.

I knew I was more than capable to do better because during the final 1km, I could still run strongly with long stride.

Overall, it was a good experience to run on hilly Padang Merbok route.

Until next time !

7 Habits of Highly Effective People

  • by ashrafm
  • on Tue 21 February 2017

1. Be proactive Focus your energy on what you can control – your circle of influence. Things that are out of your control (your circle of concerns) should be the least priority in your mind.

2. Begin with the end in mind This is the big picture view of everything. You do something because it’s related to your end goal. Having end goal in mind lets you prioritize your task. The goal helps you to creatively develop and find the “required” tasks to achieve your goals.

3. Put first things first All the tasks that you perform can be categorized into 4 quadrants. The priority of the tasks should be in the following order : Not urgent, but important > Urgent, important > Urgent, not important > Not urgent, not important

4. Think win/win This is the ultimate relationship goal. All involved parties benefit from the relationship. The relationship flourishes and all involved parties would do anything to keep the relationship going.

5. Seek first to understand, then to be understood This is the key to the effective communication. You have to listen and understand your audience before you try to deliver your message. Once you understand their reasons from their perspective, then you can adapt your message to their situation so that you can be understood.

6. Synergize This refers to putting together all the available resources and optimize their capabilities to achieve the best possible output. In simple words- work together, complement each other, strategize and optimize the capabilities .

WordPress + Flask + Apache

  • by ashrafm
  • on Wed 25 January 2017

Problem :

You have a WordPress site, with Apache 2 server. How do you run Flask application alongside your WordPress site ?

Solution :

I assume that you have already created python program for your flask app. Let say your main python flask app filename is testapp.py

1. Get wsgi mod for Apache web server. Use package installer.

In Ubuntu case :

apt install libapache2-mod-wsgi

What is WSGI (web server gateway interface) ? It’s how your webserver ‘talks’ to your application.

App’s output/input needs some kind of connection with the webserver so that this output/input can be channeled back/to the user’s browser request. This is where WSGI comes into picture.

2. Create wsgi file for your application. This will be located at the same level as your main flask app python file because we will call our flask app from this wsgi file.

import sys from testapp import app

sys.path.insert(0,'/directory-path-to-your-app')

Note : testapp is your main python flask app file (that consists of your Flask app entry point) and app is your Flask app that you define in your testapp.py

Save this file as testapp.wsgi

3. Modify your Apache conf file. Normally it’s located in site-enabled folder at /etc/apache2/ Append the following within <VirtualHost></VirtualHost> tags.

WSGIScriptAlias /flaskroot /directory-path-to-your-testapp.wsgi
<Directory /directory-path-to-your-app>
         Require all granted
</Directory>

Note: flaskroot is the root link where your app will be accessed from the browser. It can be any name/link but obviously it can not be at the same location as your WordPress homepage link or other links that are generated by the WordPress (blog posts, pages, categories)

This is the simplest way to modify your Apache conf file. If you need more secured way to do this, you can follow flask apache deployment

4. Reload your Apache server

sudo service apache2 reload

5. Try to access your flask app via the link on your browser to see if what you have done is working.

Let say you define helloworld function in testapp.py that prints out strings in your browser.

Based on this example here, you can access the app as follows : http://your-site/flaskroot/helloworld

The FORD Method

  • by ashrafm
  • on Wed 25 January 2017

Original video by improvement pill on how to keep the conversation flowing. Basically, these are the topics during the conversation. These topics can go deep and can create “emotional touch” between you and the other person.

This is part of my note collection and commentary.

F. Family.

Start with your own family. Once you open up, redirect the family related question to the other person. This way he/she wont feel reluctant to talk

Example : I live in a big family. Arguing with siblings has become part of my daily life. How about you? How many siblings do you have?

O. Occupation.

Can use the technique of opening up first and then ask related question. Comment on his/her answer and then ask question. Do not ask question upon question with no commentaries in between questions. You will sound like a police !

R. Recreation.

Try to connect with him/her with his/her hobbies. For example – How I wish I can take photos like you did !! Teach me your secret!

D. Dreams.

Don’t go directly to dream questions. Use previous 3 topics to lead to dream questions.

I notice that there are some common techniques when talking about these topics :

  1. Open up first and then ask related questions

  2. Comment on their answers before asking next questions.

  3. Make “positive assumption” statement about the person to get the conversation flowing. (Cold reading technique) E.g. from the way you sit, I believe you are a very disciplined person.

The Miracle of Linux Live CD

I wiped clean my Linux partition on my hard disk. I never thought there would be any problem. To my horror, when I tried to boot my laptop, I couldn’t even get into my Windows at all. I received Grub Rescue prompt during booting up. Trying some tips from the Internet to no avail.

Solution:

  1. Luckily, there was a Ubuntu Mate live CD within reach.

  2. Changed boot order to boot from live CD

  3. From the command line, run boot-repair program but still no luck.

  4. Re-installed back Ubuntu Mate on my previous partition.

  5. Now, I could access my Windows back during booting up!

  6. This was not the best solution but still accomplished my objective to access my Windows. I didn’t have any Windows Repair media with me. Tried to create one but failed. Don’t know why.

Lessons Learnt:

  1. Do not simply erase Linux partition. You might accidentally erase Grub. Find out more about your Grub boot loader’s location before wiping your Linux partition.

  2. Make sure you have your Linux live CD with you. With this CD, you can still access the contents of your hard disk in case you couldn’t boot using your hard disk.

7 Actions For Fast Language Acquisition

  1. Listen a lot. He called this listening process – brain soaking. Doesn’t matter if you understand them or not. Just keep listening to the spoken language – conversations, songs, videos. You unconsciously expose yourself to the structures of the language

  2. Focus on getting the meaning first (before the words). Aim for comprehension. Try to understand through the body language.

  3. Start mixing the words that you already know.  Make variety of known words combinations to communicate

  4. Focus on the core. If the language has 10000 words,  perhaps only 2000 – 3000 that matters i.e. that are regularly used. This is the core. Focus on this collection.

  5. Get a language parent. Find a native speaker friend that can guide and give feedback

  6. Copy the face. See how the native speaker speaks the words and follow how they say them.

  7. Direct connect to mental images. When you try to memorize any new word, form a mental picture of that word and make it multisensory experience (in your head!)

How To Learn Any Language in 6 Months

  • by ashrafm
  • on Tue 17 January 2017

​Notes from the TEDx talk @ Lingnan University by Chris Lonsdale

Youtube link : How To Learn Any Language in 6 Months video

This my commentary/notes based on that talk. Great talk. Check it out.

The title of the video to me is a bit hype. Doable if you put the time and effort. Regardless how fast you learn – 6 months or more, the principles and actions are solid and applicable to learn almost anything.

Some of the key points :

1- Modelling. This is a powerful concept of achieving something. Observe what works and follow them. Use the working model as your base to get started. Improvise/adjust accordingly based on the results feedback.

2- Attention, relevance, meaning and memory are all interrelated

3- We master tools when they are the most relevant to us i.e. that we always use

5 Principles of Fast Language Learning

1- Focus on language content that is relevant to you

Language is a very broad domain. You’ll be overwhelmed by the vastness of the topics to be covered when  learning new language. Most of the time, this overwhelming feeling will demotivate and lead to inaction.

Focusing on the relevance will remove the ‘noise’ and lead to faster comprehension and longer memory retention.

2- Use the language as a tool to communicate

Communication is the best way to master any language. You don’t get good just by writing or talking to yourself.

You need to go out there and try to deliver your message using your new language, assisted by whatever means you have to get the other side to understand you. This includes body language, hand gestures, drawing, etc.

It doesn’t matter if you stumble a lot at the beginning. That’s good. It means you are trying to get your message across.

Babies are not perfect communicators but they surely manage to get the parents understand them. You are just like them at this early stage.

3- When you understand the message, you will unconsciously acquire the language

Comprehension will strengthen your language acquisation and retention. Aim to understand the message, no matter how different is the new language sentence’s structure compared to your mother tongue’s

4- Physiological training

Move your mouth. Say it outloud. Flex your tongue. Shout. Curse outloud. Ok that last part is a bit too much.

5- Emotional states affect learning

Learn when you are in positive mood – happy, excited, grateful, etc. Better retention. Stressfull learning process will distance you away from what you are trying to learn.

Show Only Excerpt of Full Post On Homepage

  • by ashrafm
  • on Mon 16 January 2017

Objective :

To prevent displaying full post on homepage/archive/category/tag pages. Display only excerpt of the full post on these pages.

From user’s perspective, they don’t want to scroll each full post on these pages. They want to read only the one they choose. What’s the point of displaying full post for all the posts on these types of pages ?

Solutions :

1. Manually break the post

If you use editor in WordPress, you will notice a horizontal symbol in the editor’s toolbar. You can break your post by placing this break anywhere you want in the post.

On homepage/archive/category/tag pages, only portion before the break will be displayed. No more full post on these pages. Full post will only be shown when you go directly to the post page.

The drawback – you have to do this every time you write a new post unless you find a way to automate the process.

2. Use the_excerpt()

On homepage/archive/category/tag pages, you’ll normally find a have_posts() loop to list down all the posts on these pages. After the_post() line, you can add the_excerpt().

The excerpt doesn’t give the title post – only an excerpt of the post and link to continue reading. You need to add the title of the post.

I have done that below. You might want to add extras like dates, categories, tags – depending on what you want to have listed for each post.

<?php<br /> while ( have_posts() ) :<br /> the_post(); ?><br /> <h2><a href="<?php the_permalink(); ?>"><?php the_title() ?></a></h2><br /> <?php<br /> the_excerpt();<br /> endwhile; ?>

UPDATE

Apparantly, after studying the template codes, there is a much easier way to get around this. This is a theme-dependent code, hence please have a look at your code. I’m working on Twenty Seventeen theme.

Firstly, remove all the amendments from previous method.

Please find this line in index.php and archive.php :

get_template_part( 'template-parts/post/content', get_post_format() );

If you look into template-parts/post/ folder, you will notice that the brilliant theme developers have already done this for you. They have already created template for excerpt type listing.

What you need to do is, change the above line to this :

get_template_part( 'template-parts/post/content-excerpt', get_post_format() );

See? Just a small edit ! (^_^)
