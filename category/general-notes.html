3 main types of the hand gesture, each with different type of influence.

Use it depending on the situation or intention of your talk :

1. Palm face up – to be more persuasive, to influence the crowd to accept the presented idea

2. Palm face down – to show power and authority, to subtly exert power over the audience to “follow” your idea

3. Finger pointing – worst of all, don’t use it. It’s a rude way to tell something to someone.

7 Habits of Highly Effective People

  • by ashrafm
  • on Tue 21 February 2017

1. Be proactive Focus your energy on what you can control – your circle of influence. Things that are out of your control (your circle of concerns) should be the least priority in your mind.

2. Begin with the end in mind This is the big picture view of everything. You do something because it’s related to your end goal. Having end goal in mind lets you prioritize your task. The goal helps you to creatively develop and find the “required” tasks to achieve your goals.

3. Put first things first All the tasks that you perform can be categorized into 4 quadrants. The priority of the tasks should be in the following order : Not urgent, but important > Urgent, important > Urgent, not important > Not urgent, not important

4. Think win/win This is the ultimate relationship goal. All involved parties benefit from the relationship. The relationship flourishes and all involved parties would do anything to keep the relationship going.

5. Seek first to understand, then to be understood This is the key to the effective communication. You have to listen and understand your audience before you try to deliver your message. Once you understand their reasons from their perspective, then you can adapt your message to their situation so that you can be understood.

6. Synergize This refers to putting together all the available resources and optimize their capabilities to achieve the best possible output. In simple words- work together, complement each other, strategize and optimize the capabilities .

The FORD Method

  • by ashrafm
  • on Wed 25 January 2017

Original video by improvement pill on how to keep the conversation flowing. Basically, these are the topics during the conversation. These topics can go deep and can create “emotional touch” between you and the other person.

This is part of my note collection and commentary.

F. Family.

Start with your own family. Once you open up, redirect the family related question to the other person. This way he/she wont feel reluctant to talk

Example : I live in a big family. Arguing with siblings has become part of my daily life. How about you? How many siblings do you have?

O. Occupation.

Can use the technique of opening up first and then ask related question. Comment on his/her answer and then ask question. Do not ask question upon question with no commentaries in between questions. You will sound like a police !

R. Recreation.

Try to connect with him/her with his/her hobbies. For example – How I wish I can take photos like you did !! Teach me your secret!

D. Dreams.

Don’t go directly to dream questions. Use previous 3 topics to lead to dream questions.

I notice that there are some common techniques when talking about these topics :

  1. Open up first and then ask related questions

  2. Comment on their answers before asking next questions.

  3. Make “positive assumption” statement about the person to get the conversation flowing. (Cold reading technique) E.g. from the way you sit, I believe you are a very disciplined person.

The Miracle of Linux Live CD

  • by ashrafm
  • on Fri 20 January 2017

Problem:

I wiped clean my Linux partition on my hard disk. I never thought there would be any problem. To my horror, when I tried to boot my laptop, I couldn’t even get into my Windows at all. I received Grub Rescue prompt during booting up. Trying some tips from the Internet to no avail.

Solution:

  1. Luckily, there was a Ubuntu Mate live CD within reach.

  2. Changed boot order to boot from live CD

  3. From the command line, run boot-repair program but still no luck.

  4. Re-installed back Ubuntu Mate on my previous partition.

  5. Now, I could access my Windows back during booting up!

  6. This was not the best solution but still accomplished my objective to access my Windows. I didn’t have any Windows Repair media with me. Tried to create one but failed. Don’t know why.

Lessons Learnt:

  1. Do not simply erase Linux partition. You might accidentally erase Grub. Find out more about your Grub boot loader’s location before wiping your Linux partition.

  2. Make sure you have your Linux live CD with you. With this CD, you can still access the contents of your hard disk in case you couldn’t boot using your hard disk.

The Feynman Technique

  • by ashrafm
  • on Thu 19 January 2017

Who is Richard Feynmann ?

A famous physicist who won Nobel prize. He was really a funny guy. You should read or listen to his book – You Must Be Joking Mr Feynman. Great book !

The Feynman Technique

1. Find a topic. Study it.

2. Explain the topic either by writing it on paper or verbal explanation to your friends using your own words.

For the best result, write and talk about the topic. If you have no one to explain to, talk to yourself while imagining you are teaching someone.

3. Simplify the explanation If you truly understand the subject, you will have no problem to explain it in simpler way.

Cut the words. Use analogy. Use drawings. Explain to your audience like they are kids.

How To Learn Any Language in 6 Months - Part 2

  • by ashrafm
  • on Thu 19 January 2017

7 Actions For Fast Language Acquisition

  1. Listen a lot. He called this listening process – brain soaking. Doesn’t matter if you understand them or not. Just keep listening to the spoken language – conversations, songs, videos. You unconsciously expose yourself to the structures of the language

  2. Focus on getting the meaning first (before the words). Aim for comprehension. Try to understand through the body language.

  3. Start mixing the words that you already know.  Make variety of known words combinations to communicate

  4. Focus on the core. If the language has 10000 words,  perhaps only 2000 – 3000 that matters i.e. that are regularly used. This is the core. Focus on this collection.

  5. Get a language parent. Find a native speaker friend that can guide and give feedback

  6. Copy the face. See how the native speaker speaks the words and follow how they say them.

  7. Direct connect to mental images. When you try to memorize any new word, form a mental picture of that word and make it multisensory experience (in your head!)

How To Learn Any Language in 6 Months

  • by ashrafm
  • on Tue 17 January 2017

​Notes from the TEDx talk @ Lingnan University by Chris Lonsdale

Youtube link : How To Learn Any Language in 6 Months video

This my commentary/notes based on that talk. Great talk. Check it out.

The title of the video to me is a bit hype. Doable if you put the time and effort. Regardless how fast you learn – 6 months or more, the principles and actions are solid and applicable to learn almost anything.

Some of the key points :

1- Modelling. This is a powerful concept of achieving something. Observe what works and follow them. Use the working model as your base to get started. Improvise/adjust accordingly based on the results feedback.

2- Attention, relevance, meaning and memory are all interrelated

3- We master tools when they are the most relevant to us i.e. that we always use

5 Principles of Fast Language Learning

1- Focus on language content that is relevant to you

Language is a very broad domain. You’ll be overwhelmed by the vastness of the topics to be covered when  learning new language. Most of the time, this overwhelming feeling will demotivate and lead to inaction.

Focusing on the relevance will remove the ‘noise’ and lead to faster comprehension and longer memory retention.

2- Use the language as a tool to communicate

Communication is the best way to master any language. You don’t get good just by writing or talking to yourself.

You need to go out there and try to deliver your message using your new language, assisted by whatever means you have to get the other side to understand you. This includes body language, hand gestures, drawing, etc.

It doesn’t matter if you stumble a lot at the beginning. That’s good. It means you are trying to get your message across.

Babies are not perfect communicators but they surely manage to get the parents understand them. You are just like them at this early stage.

3- When you understand the message, you will unconsciously acquire the language

Comprehension will strengthen your language acquisation and retention. Aim to understand the message, no matter how different is the new language sentence’s structure compared to your mother tongue’s

4- Physiological training

Move your mouth. Say it outloud. Flex your tongue. Shout. Curse outloud. Ok that last part is a bit too much.

5- Emotional states affect learning

Learn when you are in positive mood – happy, excited, grateful, etc. Better retention. Stressfull learning process will distance you away from what you are trying to learn.
